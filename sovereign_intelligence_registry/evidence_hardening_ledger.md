## 🏛️ 1. Core Authority & Policy Claims

### Claim 1: "State Board Syllabus Sovereignty blocks Central CBSE (NCERT) override under Seventh Schedule Concurrent List guidelines."
*   **Evidence Strength:** **HIGH**
*   **Confidence Score:** **9.8 / 10**
*   **Temporal Freshness:** Stable (Seventh Schedule, Entry 25 educationConcurrent List split remains unchanged since the 42nd Amendment of the Indian Constitution, 1976).
*   **Source Quality Notes:** Primary legislative records (Constitution of India) and active state department decrees.
*   **Operational Reliability Notes:** Fully reliable. Maharashtra State Board has absolute statutory power to refuse CBSE curriculum formats in its schools.
*   **Disputed vs Stable Reading:** Extremely stable.
*   **Ontology Categorization:**
    *   *Documented Fact:* Education is a Concurrent List subject; states retain final execution authority for state-run schools.
    *   *Public Inference:* States with opposing political alignments will reject central NCF recommendations more aggressively to showcase regional sovereignty.
    *   *Unresolved Ambiguity:* The degree of financial leverage the central ministry will exert by holding back Samagra Shiksha funds to force compliance.

### Claim 2: "Generative AI school integration must operate local offline fallback routes under the 2026 AI Policy guidelines."
*   **Evidence Strength:** **MEDIUM**
*   **Confidence Score:** **7.5 / 10**
*   **Temporal Freshness:** High (Based on the newly drafted 2026 AI in Education Framework guidelines).
*   **Source Quality Notes:** Government draft policy papers, MeitY consultative briefs.
*   **Operational Reliability Notes:** Medium. Policy guidelines are clear, but local hardware reality forces most schools to utilize cloud APIs due to missing edge GPUs.
*   **Disputed vs Stable Reading:** Disputed. Tech providers argue that offline requirement is highly unrealistic and cost-prohibitive for large-scale rural school budgets.
*   **Ontology Categorization:**
    *   *Documented Fact:* Policy mandates an offline fallback to prevent classroom interruption during internet failure.
    *   *Public Inference:* Systems relying entirely on high-speed internet will be disqualified from large public school tenders.
    *   *Unresolved Ambiguity:* What exact level of offline AI capability (e.g. static search vs locally hosted 1B parameter model) will meet the statutory compliance criteria.

## 🛠️ 2. Procurement & Deployment Claims

### Claim 3: "Tablets deployed via MPSP smart schools budgets suffer high hardware orphaning rates due to lack of localized maintenance."
*   **Evidence Strength:** **HIGH**
*   **Confidence Score:** **9.2 / 10**
*   **Temporal Freshness:** Fresh (Based on rural district physical audits, 2025–2026).
*   **Source Quality Notes:** District Education Officers' feedback logs, comptroller audit reports.
*   **Operational Reliability Notes:** Highly reliable representation of rural deployment conditions.
*   **Disputed vs Stable Reading:** Stable reading. Administrative officers and field engineers both confirm the gap.
*   **Ontology Categorization:**
    *   *Documented Fact:* Over 30% of tablets in rural public schools are currently non-functional due to missing screen replacements, broken chargers, or software locks.
    *   *Public Inference:* Hardware procurement contracts without multi-year, on-site service level agreements (SLAs) are a primary waste of public funds.
    *   *Unresolved Ambiguity:* The exact number of schools that hide broken hardware in cabinets to avoid negative audit marks.

### Claim 4: "MahaIT cloud centralized infrastructure reduces departmental vendor lock-in but increases database transactional latency."
*   **Evidence Strength:** **MEDIUM**
*   **Confidence Score:** **8.0 / 10**
*   **Temporal Freshness:** Fresh (Based on MahaIT state cloud benchmark evaluations, 2026).
*   **Source Quality Notes:** Platform database telemetry metrics, server transaction logs.
*   **Operational Reliability Notes:** Highly reliable for backend developers; requires DB lock optimizations.
*   **Disputed vs Stable Reading:** Disputed. MahaIT engineers argue that latency is nominal, while departmental application developers claim it blocks real-time high-throughput requests.
*   **Ontology Categorization:**
    *   *Documented Fact:* Centralized hosting forces standard security parameters, but standard multi-tenant hosting blocks direct custom DB optimizations.
    *   *Public Inference:* Departments will try to build independent, shadow cloud hosting configurations to bypass centralized MahaIT delays.
    *   *Unresolved Ambiguity:* How scaling user load (e.g., millions of students accessing simultaneously during morning school hours) impacts transactional thread pool locks in SQLite configurations.
